The MODFLOW groundwater modeling software code was originally developed by the United States Geological Survey (USGS) in the early 1980s to create a single program combining the best capabilities of various codes used in USGS studies \citep{mcdonald2003}. Major MODFLOW releases were developed internally by the USGS and released approximately every 4 to 8 years \citep{modflow84, modflow88, harbaugh1996user, hill2000modflow, modflow2005}. Eventually, non-USGS copntributions were incorporated into MODFLOW \citep[for example,][]{hoffmann2003modflow, wilson2004modflow, bakker2013documentation} or MODFLOW variants \citep[e.g.,][]{modflowvsf, modflownwt, modflowusg}.

MODFLOW 6, the latest version of MODFLOW, was released in 2017 and consolidated popular capabilities available in MODFLOW variants \citep{modflow6gwf}. MODFLOW capabilities were quickly extended with the addition of groundwater transport \citep{modflow6gwt}, groundwater energy \citep{morway2025new}, and particle tracking models. 

As of 2026, the MODFLOW core supports incorporating additional hydrologic processes, allows interaction among processes, multiple model instances, multiple types of models within a single simulation, parallel simulation, and interoperability that allows external programs to access and control MODFLOW \citep{langevin2024modflow}. Unlike previous versions of MODFLOW, modern open-source software development approaches and direct collaboration with external partners (e.g., Deltares, University Corporation for Atmospheric Research, Delft University of Technology, etc.\@) has accelerated development. In this modern approach, the core is maintained with disparate branches inheriting it and dynamically building from it. This differs from previous codes like MODFLOW-OWHM and MODFLOW-SURFACT \citep{boyce2020, panday2008modflow} that were added statically to previous MODFLOW versions. Once that hard fork was made, the connection was broken. Maintaining this core, collaboratively, is a key modern software design precept and the highest priority to ensure longevity and innovation of MODFLOW going forward.

In 2025, there was approximately a 20\% reduction in USGS staff due to the new administration's initiatives and the Deferred Resignation Program. Several key MODFLOW developers left the USGS for private industry. Naturally, this led to many MODFLOW users and skeptics wondering if MODFLOW development and support for the core would continue. To address this, the USGS and core MODFLOW contributors developed a \href{https://github.com/MODFLOW-ORG/modflow6/wiki/MODFLOW-Governance-Charter}{MODFLOW Governance Charter} to further broaden the development and decision-making base to formally include a broader cross-section of partners from the private and academic sectors of the MODFLOW community, particularly at the technical level. The Governance enables unfettered innovation in a way that also keeps the MODFLOW core functional for all needs, mitigating technical conflicts as they arise.

This charter defines MODFLOW as an open-source core framework for government, private, and academic use. While the USGS retains final authority over formal releases and high-level strategy, MODFLOW is now technically guided by a Technical Steering Committee (TSC).
The TSC oversees software improvements, new features, documentation, and training. It consists of at least two USGS developers and two community members who serve renewable three-year terms. Membership and document changes are managed through a majority vote, ensuring a collaborative approach to long-term maintenance. TSC members are required to have 1) a track record of contributing to MODFLOW development, 2) advanced technical skills directly relevant to core development, such as proficiency in the underlying model framework or related technical functions, and 3) an ongoing ability and willingness to participate in both day-to-day development and high-level decision-making. The current TSC membership is made up of the authors of this Editorial and are listed at \href{https://github.com/MODFLOW-ORG/modflow6/wiki/Technical-Steering-Committee-Meetings}{MODFLOW TSC Members}.

With USGS management approval of and support for the charter, we are confident that MODFLOW development and maintenance will continue. Furthermore, the charter provides a defined mechanism for continued development and improvement in addition to succession of new TSC members when current members resign, are voted out, or retire. 

2025 was a tumultuous year for MODFLOW, but the path forward is strong. The TSC provides continuity, with ongoing development from the core team while providing new opportunities for community involvement and direction. The result will be a core MODFLOW code that serves many different needs and is improved intentionally and robustly into the future.
